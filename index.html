Projects

Continuum

2023 - Present

Continuum is a minimal iOS app and widget that shows you the % of your day that has passed and the % of your day that is left.

I built it as a way to learn SwiftUI and as simple reminder to myself that I have more time in my day than I realise, and that I can make use of it.

pints.wtf

April 2019 - 2021

Pints.wtf is a viral website that ranks drinks in Wetherspoons pubs by their value for alcohol.

It's been used by over 100K users since its launch, and was featured in The Tab, The Daily Mail, The Sun, The Mirror, BBC Radio, and LADBible.

I reverse engineered the Wetherspoons app to access drink price data, and use a simple Flask server to calculate the ranking (unit alcohol per GBP) for any given pub.

Sadly, in 2021 I forgot to update my expired credit card info on the domain registrar and the site was unjustly sold on auction. To this day, I am still bitter about that (2021 - Present).

Movemaison

Autumn 2018

Movemaison was a platform to find student housing in Oxford. It attempted to address the difficulty students had in exploring a range of housing options from different estate agents due to poor user-experience.

It was originally designed to also function as a review platform of estate agents and landlords. It was a great learning exercise and let me explore a lot of technologies for the first time.

Stack used:

  • React
  • Relay (GraphQL) map-search functionality
  • Django server with a Graphene API
  • Deployed via Heroku

Breathewise

Spring 2019

Breathewise was the end result of a group project as part of the Computer Science course at Oxford. I led a team of 5 CS undergrads to develop a application to visualise pollution levels across London based on data from an API provided by our sponsor, Breathe London.

Features:

  • React front end
  • Flask API server
  • Custom data compression
  • Routing between two locations so users could choose the route with the lowest levels of pollution
  • Displayed levels of different pollutants for different times of the day/week at a 25m2 resolution of London
